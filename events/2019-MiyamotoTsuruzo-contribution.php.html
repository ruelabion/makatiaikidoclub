How to deposit your early-bird (or late-bird) payment:
If you are not a millennial who's used to electronic banking, or otherwise not yet comfortable with the new conveniences of personal banking, here is your opportunity to learn these convenient payment methods and improve your quality of life way beyond this seminar, and way beyond the aikido passion.

1) Paying online: If your bank offers online banking, register your account online (or visit your branch if necessary). There are simple procedures that you only ever do once; then you can transfer money online to the BPI bank account cited above. (Your bank does not have to be BPI.) Once you go through this procedure the first time, you can't help but see how easy it is; and your banking transactions for other aspects of your life will be eased henceforth beyond this aikido seminar. (NOTE: Please Viber or email a copy of the bank confirmation or machine stub receipt of the payment to the contact numbers cited above.)

2) Paying via deposit machines in-branch: BPI and other banks have specialized deposit-taking machines in many branches, allowing anyone to deposit cash without having to line up to deal with the human tellers. This is a huge time-saver. Again, the procedure for depositing into the machine is very simple; and once you've done it the first time, it will be a convenience that's available to you for other aspects of your banking life. (NOTE: Please Viber or email a copy of the bank confirmation or machine stub receipt of the payment to the contact numbers cited above.)

3) Paying the old-school way: If both of the above methods are still too complicated for you, you may pay old-fashioned cash to the designated officer or sensei of your organization, who will take responsibility for conveying the payment to the seminar project organizers. (NOTE: The receiving officer should, upon depositing the payment, likewise Viber or email a copy of the bank confirmation or machine stub receipt of the payment to the contact numbers cited above.)
